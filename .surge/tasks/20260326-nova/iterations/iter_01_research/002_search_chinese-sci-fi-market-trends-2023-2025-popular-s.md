## Chinese Sci-Fi Market Overview (2023–2025)

The Chinese sci-fi industry surpassed **¥108.96 billion (~$15B USD)** in total value, marking continued growth. Several reports, including the *2025 China Sci-Fi Industry Report*, highlight the following trends:

---

### 1. Rise of "Sci-Fi+" Hybrid Subgenres
The dominant trend is **genre fusion** ("科幻+"):
- **Sci-Fi × Traditional Culture ("Silk-Punk")**: Works like *《金桃》* blend Tang Dynasty aesthetics with futuristic narratives, and *《土楼飞碟》* merges Fujian Hakka architecture with alien themes. This "silk-punk" subgenre is uniquely Chinese and backed by local policy in cities like Beijing and Chengdu.
- **Sci-Fi × Wuxia/Fantasy**: Web novels like *《诡秘之主》 (Lord of the Mysteries)* combine sci-fi with martial arts, suspense, and historical elements — hugely popular with younger online readers.
- **Sci-Fi × Coming-of-Age**: Stories like *《我们生活在南京》 (We Live in Nanjing)* blend sci-fi with personal growth themes.

---

### 2. Web Fiction & Digital Ecosystem
- **Web literature platforms** are a primary driver of new sci-fi subgenres, with 2024 described as a year of "diverse type innovation and frequent new themes."
- Popular web fiction categories include **future history, sci-fi wuxia, mecha, and post-apocalyptic survival**.
- The digital ecosystem is described as "collaboratively advancing" with domestic creative forces, making web novels a launchpad for adaptations into games, anime, and short dramas.

---

### 3. Youth & Gen Z Audiences
- **"Soft sci-fi"** with **二次元 (ACG/anime) aesthetics** resonates strongly with Gen Z — e.g., the anime *《灵笼》 (Ling Long)*.
- **Micro-short dramas (微短剧)** — AI-generated series like *《太阳坠落之时》* and *《我在月球当包工头》* — are popular on short-video platforms, featuring themes like superpowers, space exploration, and localized humor.
- Young readers gravitate toward **eco-dystopian** and **AI ethics** narratives reflecting real-world anxieties about technology and climate.

---

### 4. Sci-Fi Games Boom
- Chinese indie games are challenging Western dominance: titles like *《无限机兵》* (souls-like mecha) and *《命运：群星》* (mobile space strategy) are expanding the sci-fi audience to casual players.

---

### 5. Globalization ("文化出海")
- Chinese sci-fi is going global, with strong demand in **Brazil and Latin America** for translated novels and sci-fi games like *《群星纪元》*.
- Immersive tech (VR films like *《三体・四维空间》*, AR merchandise) is pushing storytelling innovation.

---

### Three Macro Trends Identified by State Media (CCTV, Guangming Daily):
1. **Tech Breakthroughs** — AI-generated content (AIGC), VR/AR integration
2. **Ecosystem Optimization** — policy support, cross-media IP development
3. **Cultural Export** — international distribution of novels, films, and games
