# Source 1: Sixth Tone — "What Is Silkpunk?"

## Definition
Silkpunk represents a fusion of "silk — an aesthetic style inspired by Asian-Pacific mythology, history, and culture" with "a punk-like tendency to question, deconstruct, and rebel." Similar to cyberpunk and steampunk, it challenges established power structures while centering Asian-Pacific traditions.

## Core Philosophy
The genre seeks to "excavate the buried roots of tradition and exorcise the ghosts of colonialism from the Asia-Pacific region." It explicitly rejects both ethnocentric representation and indiscriminate cultural appropriation, instead creating hybrid narratives that blend multiple literary traditions.

## Key Works
- **"Dandelion Dynasty" series** by Ken Liu (2015 onwards) — the primary exemplar, spanning three generations across an imagined archipelago
- **"Good Hunting"** (2012) — adapted into a Netflix "Love, Death & Robots" episode
- **"Paper Menagerie"** — Hugo Award winner

## Visual and Cultural Elements
The Dandelion Dynasty features silk wings, luminous whales, culinary competitions, shamanic rituals, and animal-form deities. The aesthetic incorporates "bones, shell, bamboo, and, of course, silk," merging Asian-Pacific imagery with speculative fiction.

## Literary Approach
Liu blends classical sources (Milton, Homer, Chaucer) with Eastern traditions ("Romance of the Three Kingdoms," Chinese ghost stories), creating what the author terms "cosmonarratology"—a cosmopolitan narrative style transcending cultural boundaries.

# Source 2: The Portalist — "Silkpunk Books"

## Core Definition
Silkpunk is a science fiction/fantasy subgenre formally defined by Ken Liu in 2015. Rather than simply referring to "any work influenced by Asian culture," it has specific criteria: "form is as important as function" with emphasis on technology, engineering, poetry, and language.

## Key Aesthetic Elements

**Technology & Materials:**
The subgenre features organic-based devices—bamboo, silk, and shells—that maintain natural appearances while serving technological purposes. This contrasts sharply with steampunk's industrial metalwork.

**Thematic Core:**
Silkpunk blends "themes of resistance, rebellion, and revolution" with Asian cultural storytelling traditions, particularly East Asian narrative forms that romanticize historical drama.

**Literary Fusion:**
Works combine Western epic fantasy structures with East Asian romance traditions (exemplified by *Romance of the Three Kingdoms*), creating hybrid storytelling approaches.

## Foundational Works

**The Grace of Kings** (Ken Liu) - The definitive silkpunk novel introducing the Dandelion Dynasty series, featuring rival leaders whose friendship fractures over conflicting ideals of justice.

**Other Notable Titles:**
- *Celestial Matters* (Richard Garfinkle) - Retroactively labeled silkpunk
- *The Black Tides of Heaven* (JY Yang) - Twin protagonists with extraordinary abilities
- *Monstress* (Marjorie Liu/Sana Takeda) - Graphic novel set in early 20th-century Asian-inspired world
- *Rebel Skies* (Ann Sei Lin) - Features paper spirit manipulation
- *Gunpowder Alchemy* (Jeannie Lin) - Chinese technology-based alternate history
